    To take an input from the physical world and place it into the arduino so in the future we could map these to drum sounds we needed something that detected vibrations. Pieszos worked perfectly for this because when hit with a stick (such as you would with a drum kit) it produces an output.

    We created this circuit connected to a buzzer.

    Using this code we took an input from the pieszo and outputted it to the buzzer

    Now we wanted to have two inputs into our drum kit. One for the kick and one for the snare. We also wanted to have two potentiometers that would control certain aspects of the sounds. One potentiometer would control the volume of the kit, and the other would vary the sounds being played. For the code we connected processing to Arduino. This allowed us to produce sounds from outputted to signals.

    Soldering the Protoboard

    Click to edit text

    The protoboard would be simple and easy. All that was needed was connection to ground for the potentiometers and pieszos, connection to power for the potentiometers, and outputs for the potentiometers and pieszos. Our goal was to make the protoboard as small as possible in order for it to fit int the enclosure. In the top left of the protoboard is connection to ground. Bottom left is connection to power. And in the middle are 1 million resistors for the pieszo outputs.

    With our enclosure we wanted something sturdy that could take hits from the drum sticks so we chose to make it out of wood. Unfortanetly after making this decision we found out the CNC machine was broken, so our entire enclosure was hand made. First we started with this outline. ---->


    Click to edit text

    Then we started to drill out parts where the pieces would go. The back would hold the arduino, all the wires, and the protoboard. This is what it looked like after drilling out these parts. Without the cnc machine we struggled with making the enclosure neat. Luckily this was the back part and would be hidden from the user.

    To make the pieszo sensors' surface areas larger we glued them to old cd's. Then we covered them with fabric to soften the hit and make it more aesthetically pleasing.
